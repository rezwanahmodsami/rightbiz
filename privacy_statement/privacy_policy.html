Privacy Policy & GDPR

  • Protecting your privacy is one of our highest priorities. We developed this policy to demonstrate our firm commitment to protecting you and your privacy. Please, read the following information to learn more.

  • On the date of 25th May 2018 and onwards, in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Individual's rights include:

  • Right to be informed: An individual will be required to provide personal data which Rightbiz Ltd will then store in its database. Data which are stored include the following: name, address/location, contact number, email address, interested business sectors, social networking service is used if registering via a social login and all the relevant information that is required to publish a business for sale advert; such as business details, photos if provided and additional documents.

  • Personal data is held for the purpose of supplying the individual with information that possibly is an interest to them, additionally with details that are progressing a transaction if suitable, and/or if a member of the staff of Rightbiz Ltd is required to contact them. Provided data is held by Rightbiz Ltd on secure servers for internal use, and is not shared with any Third Parties for marketing purposes. The data will be protected using appropriate security methods and will only obtain appropriate consents for processing data.

  • If the individual uses Rightbiz Ltd to publish a business for sale advert or chooses to contact a business for sale advert, details will be exchanged to the enquirer or the enquired in order to assist further the sale of a business. The particulars exchanged are as follows: email address, name, contact number and possibly address. Details are only exchanged to Rightbiz Ltd members.

  • Right to access: Every registered member of Rightbiz Ltd can access their personal data via their members' control panel on www.rightbiz.co.uk. If particular data are not found or not displayed, then these details can be requested using the contact form on www.rightbiz.co.uk/contact_us/ and subsequently information will be provided by a member of Rightbiz Ltd staff. Requests are free of charge with the exception of where requests are noticeably unfounded or excessive; we then hold the right to charge a reasonable fee considering the administrative costs of providing the information.

  • Right to export: Individuals have the right to export their personal data, this can be requested using the contact form on www.rightbiz.co.uk/contact_us/ and subsequently the exported data will be provided by a member of Rightbiz Ltd staff. Requests are free of charge with the exception of where requests are noticeably unfounded or excessive; we then hold the right to charge a reasonable fee considering the administrative costs of providing the information.

  • Right to rectification: Individuals have the right to have personal data rectified if inaccurate or incomplete. These details can be altered via their members' control panel on www.rightbiz.co.uk. If particular data cannot be modified, then these details can be changed at request using the contact form on www.rightbiz.co.uk/contact_us/ and subsequently information will be rectified by a member of Rightbiz Ltd staff.

  • Right to erasure: Individuals have the right to deactivate their account; this can be done via their members' control panel on www.rightbiz.co.uk. However, if the individual wishes to request the deletion or removal of their personal data from our system, this can be asked using the contact form on www.rightbiz.co.uk/contact_us/ in the following circumstances:

    • Where the data is no longer necessary in regards to the purpose for which it was originally collected or processed.

    • When the individual withdraws consent.

    • When the individual objects to the processing and there is no overriding legitimate interest for continuing the processing.

    • The personal data was unlawfully processed (i.e. otherwise in breach of the GDPR).

    • The personal data has to be erased in order to comply with a legal obligation.

  • This does not provide an absolute "Right to be forgotten" as some personal data in question may have been disclosed to a third party. When requested personal data can be erased from our servers.

  • Right to object processing: Individuals have a right to object to processing of personal data if the individual contests its accuracy, has objected to the processing, processing is unlawful and the individual objects to erasure, and/or the company no longer needs the personal data but the individual requires the data to establish, exercise or defend a legal claim. This can be done by deactivating their account via their members' control panel on www.rightbiz.co.uk.

  • All requests made to Rightbiz will be dealt with in their own importance and in accordance with GDPR guidance.

  • Should a data breach occur, Rightbiz Ltd will take procedures to investigate and report the matter to the individual. In the event of a violation, it will be reported to the individual as soon as possible.
